The spanish Flu

Where did it come from? How dangerous was it? What were its effects?

The year is 1918 and the location of the earliest documented case of the spanish flu is taken place in kansas in the united states. The cause of this flu is thought to be from cow manure being burned in the air in Fort Filey. It was certain that young men leaving the fort for military service carried the virus to the battlefields in europe which would then infect hundreds of thousands more people.

The Effect The Spanish Flu took on the world was very bad. For example, the life expectancy in the United States had dropped by 12 years and it wasn't just the United States but the life expectancy also dropped for other countries besides the United States Too. The effects the Influenza had on the Soldiers in the battlefield is that it clogged transportation lines, overcrowded hospitals, killed thousands of soldiers, and rendered many soldiers non-effective. The Influenza also exhausted soldiers and made them demoralized. It was also said that the Influenza killed more American Military personnel than factors from the enemy.

Conditions of the trenches

How were the conditions in the trenches?

“Hell is not fire; that would be the ultimate in suffering. Hell is mud.” Conditions in the trenches were severely horrible. Rats and lice tormented Troops which also contributed to diseases spreading in the trenches which included Trench fever which was caused by lice which gave soldiers headaches, fevers, and muscle pain. Then there was the unsanitary conditions of being stationed in trenches which will give you trench foot which if not taken care of will lead to gangrene and amputation of the foot.
